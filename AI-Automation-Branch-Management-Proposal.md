# AI Automation & Agent Services Branch

## Six-Month Management Proposal

**Prepared for:** Management Team  
**Planning period:** Six months  
**Document date:** 13 July 2026  
**Status:** Full six-month business case for management approval

---

## 1. Executive Summary

This proposal recommends launching a new **AI Automation & Agent Services Branch** under the existing company. The branch will sell business-focused AI automation solutions through Fiverr, direct B2B outreach, existing business relationships, LinkedIn, cold email, and agency partnerships.

The branch will not sell generic chatbots or AI tools. It will sell measurable business solutions such as lead qualification, appointment booking, e-commerce customer support, CRM follow-up, and automated marketing reporting.

The recommended approach is a **six-month managed launch**:

1. Validate demand and build sellable demos.
2. Launch Fiverr and B2B acquisition.
3. Deliver paid pilot projects and build case studies.
4. Productize the successful solutions.
5. Build recurring support revenue.
6. Decide whether to scale, restructure, or stop based on evidence.

The branch may use existing company resources during the validation period, but all shared-resource usage will be recorded so that management can see both the actual cash cost and the true operating cost.

### Recommended management approval

- Approve the complete six-month branch launch plan.
- Approve a six-month operating budget ceiling of **BDT 700,000**.
- Keep up to **BDT 200,000** available as management-approved contingency for accelerated traction or unexpected infrastructure needs.
- Appoint one Branch Lead.
- Hire or assign one dedicated AI Automation Developer.
- Allow controlled access to existing design, frontend, QA, finance, legal, and infrastructure resources.
- Release the approved operating budget monthly and review progress against agreed KPIs without interrupting the six-month execution plan.

## 2. Business Objective

The objective is to build a repeatable and profitable service branch that helps international and local businesses automate revenue, support, reporting, and operational workflows.

### Six-month business outcomes

- Validate three productized AI automation solutions.
- Build at least three working demos and five strong case studies.
- Establish Fiverr and direct B2B as repeatable acquisition channels.
- Acquire 8–12 active clients across project and retainer work.
- Build recurring support revenue.
- Maintain a target gross margin of at least 50% after direct delivery costs.
- Create documented sales, delivery, security, and support SOPs.
- Make an evidence-based decision on independent staffing and infrastructure.

These are management targets, not guaranteed financial outcomes. Forecasts will be updated after real conversion and delivery data are available.

## 3. Recommended Market Position

### Primary niche

**AI Automation for E-commerce and Marketing Businesses**

This niche is recommended because it provides clear, repeatable business problems and aligns with the company's broader experience in Shopify, WordPress, Wix, SEO, Meta Ads, Google Ads, web, and application services.

### Initial productized solutions

#### Solution 1: AI Lead Qualification & Appointment Booking

- Capture website, form, email, or messaging leads.
- Ask qualification questions.
- Score and route qualified leads.
- Update the CRM or spreadsheet.
- Book appointments.
- Send automated follow-ups.

#### Solution 2: E-commerce AI Customer Support

- Answer product and policy questions.
- Assist with order-related queries.
- Collect customer information.
- Escalate complex cases to a human agent.
- Produce support summaries and reports.

#### Solution 3: Marketing Reporting & Client Operations Automation

- Collect Google Ads and Meta Ads data.
- Generate scheduled client reports.
- Summarize performance in plain language.
- Deliver reports through email or collaboration tools.
- Automate agency onboarding and recurring follow-up tasks.

Additional services should initially be sold as add-ons rather than separate offers. This will reduce sales complexity and make delivery more repeatable.

## 4. Go-to-Market Strategy

The branch will use multiple channels, but they will not receive equal effort at the beginning.

### Primary channels for Months 1–2

1. Fiverr
2. Existing business relationships and referrals
3. Direct B2B outreach through LinkedIn and cold email

### Secondary channels after validation

1. Agency white-label partnerships
2. Upwork
3. Industry groups and communities
4. Content-led inbound acquisition

### Proposed Fiverr offers

- Shopify AI customer support automation
- AI lead qualification and booking with n8n
- Automated Google Ads and Meta Ads client reporting

### Initial Fiverr pricing hypothesis

| Offer | Indicative price |
|---|---:|
| Paid discovery or automation audit | USD 50–100 |
| Starter implementation | USD 250–400 |
| Business workflow | USD 700–1,200 |
| Advanced custom system | USD 1,500–3,000+ |
| Monthly support | USD 100–500+ |

Fiverr currently credits freelancers with 80% of a completed order's value. Standard freelancer earnings are also subject to a clearing period. Marketplace deductions and payment delays must therefore be included in pricing and cash-flow planning.

### Weekly B2B activity targets after launch

- 100 researched target accounts
- 50–75 cold emails
- 25 personalized LinkedIn messages
- 10 warm or existing-relationship conversations
- 5 agency partnership pitches
- 3–5 discovery calls

All opportunities will be managed through one pipeline:

`Lead → Contacted → Replied → Qualified → Discovery → Proposal → Won/Lost → Retainer`

## 5. Initial Team Structure

| Function | Months 1–2 | Months 3–4 | Months 5–6 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Branch leadership and sales | One dedicated Branch Lead | Dedicated | Dedicated |
| AI automation development | One dedicated developer | One dedicated developer | Add capacity only if demand requires it |
| Fiverr and lead generation | Branch Lead initially | Add a dedicated junior if pipeline activity is insufficient | Dedicated channel ownership |
| Frontend/UI | Shared or project-based | Shared or allocated | Dedicated only if justified |
| QA and support | Shared plus developer | Documented QA process | Dedicated capacity if active clients require it |
| Content and demo production | Shared or project-based | Scheduled support | Channel-specific content plan |
| Finance, legal, and admin | Existing company support or allocated cost | Separate branch reporting | Independent process if branch scales |

### Hiring principle

Additional hiring will be based on demand and capacity, not only on the calendar. A second developer should be approved only when:

- The current developer is consistently above 80% capacity.
- There is at least two months of confirmed or highly probable work.
- Delivery SOPs and reusable templates exist.
- The branch has at least three months of salary reserve.
- Target gross margin remains at or above 50%.

## 6. Six-Month Execution Roadmap

### Month 1: Research and Validation

**Objective:** Identify what the market will pay for and prepare credible sales assets.

**Activities**

- Interview 20 potential or existing business contacts.
- Analyze 50–100 relevant Fiverr gigs.
- Analyze 100 recent AI automation job posts and requests.
- Document customer problems, budgets, tools, urgency, and expected outcomes.
- Select the three initial productized solutions.
- Build three working demos.
- Record three short demonstration videos.
- Prepare landing-page copy, proposal, quotation, discovery form, and onboarding checklist.
- Set up CRM stages and branch cost tracking.

**Month 1 exit criteria**

- Three validated offers
- Three functional demos
- Twenty customer conversations
- Documented competitor and demand research
- Sales and delivery assets ready

### Month 2: Fiverr and B2B Launch

**Objective:** Generate qualified conversations and secure the first paid work.

**Activities**

- Publish three Fiverr gigs.
- Begin structured LinkedIn and cold-email outreach.
- Contact warm business relationships where permitted.
- Start agency partnership outreach.
- Run discovery calls and submit proposals.
- Improve offers based on objections and response data.

**Month 2 exit criteria**

- At least 300 researched prospects contacted
- 10–15 qualified discovery calls
- 5 or more proposals
- 1–2 paid pilot projects
- At least one channel showing credible demand

### Month 3: Delivery, Proof, and Case Studies

**Objective:** Deliver successfully and turn early work into proof.

**Activities**

- Deliver pilot projects with clear acceptance criteria.
- Measure time saved, response improvement, lead conversion, or other relevant outcomes.
- Collect testimonials and client approval for case studies.
- Offer maintenance and monitoring packages.
- Start a referral process.
- Decide whether a dedicated lead-generation person is required.

**Month 3 exit criteria**

- Three completed projects
- Two published case studies
- At least one maintenance client
- Documented delivery time and project margin

### Month 4: Productization and Higher-Value Sales

**Objective:** Reduce custom work and increase average project value.

**Activities**

- Convert successful workflows into reusable templates.
- Standardize scope, milestones, testing, and handover.
- Create industry-specific versions of successful offers.
- Improve proposals with case-study evidence.
- Introduce higher-value CRM, reporting, and multi-step automation packages.

**Month 4 exit criteria**

- Delivery time reduced by approximately 30%
- Average project value trending upward
- Three or more reusable workflow modules
- Repeatable QA and deployment checklist

### Month 5: Channel Scaling and Recurring Revenue

**Objective:** Build predictable acquisition and support revenue.

**Activities**

- Focus on the two best-performing acquisition channels.
- Develop white-label agency partnerships.
- Offer support plans to every eligible client.
- Create monthly reporting and account-review processes.
- Publish case-study and educational content.
- Track channel-level acquisition cost and conversion.

**Month 5 exit criteria**

- At least five support or maintenance clients
- Three functioning acquisition channels or two strongly validated channels
- Clear channel-level performance data
- Growing monthly recurring revenue

### Month 6: Branch Validation and Scale Decision

**Objective:** Determine whether the branch is ready for independent scaling.

**Activities**

- Review complete branch P&L and cash flow.
- Compare cash cost with fully allocated operating cost.
- Identify the best-selling and most profitable offer.
- Remove low-margin or high-risk services.
- Finalize sales, delivery, security, support, and incident SOPs.
- Review developer and support capacity.
- Decide on dedicated hiring, tools, brand, and infrastructure.

**Month 6 exit criteria**

- 8–12 active project or retainer clients
- Five or more case studies
- Target gross margin of at least 50%
- Predictable lead and proposal pipeline
- Management decision: scale, continue validation, restructure, or stop

## 7. Technology and Operational Requirements

### Core technology

- n8n for workflow orchestration
- OpenAI, Claude, or Gemini APIs based on use case
- Supabase or PostgreSQL for structured data
- Vector database only where document retrieval is required
- Docker and VPS or managed hosting
- GitHub for code and version control
- CRM for sales pipeline management
- Business email and calendar
- Monitoring, error alerts, and backup
- Password and credential manager

### Operating principles

- Client credentials must not be stored in personal accounts.
- Production and demo environments must be separate.
- Every project must have backup, error handling, and ownership documentation.
- API, messaging, voice, hosting, and automation usage must have defined limits.
- Client usage cost should be billed separately or included only up to a written limit.
- Code, workflows, documentation, and credentials must remain under approved company or client ownership.

OpenAI API billing is separate from ChatGPT subscriptions and is usage-based. n8n can be used through paid cloud plans or a self-hosted Community Edition. The selected deployment model should depend on client security, collaboration, support, and execution requirements.

## 8. Budget Framework

Management should review two cost views:

1. **Cash cost:** Money paid directly by the branch.
2. **Fully allocated cost:** Cash cost plus the internal value of shared staff, office, tools, and management support.

### Scenario A: Lean validation model

| Cost item | Monthly estimate |
|---|---:|
| AI Automation Developer | BDT 35,000 |
| API, hosting, and database | BDT 10,000–20,000 |
| Outreach tools and lead data | BDT 10,000–20,000 |
| Demo, content, and marketplace expenses | BDT 5,000–10,000 |
| Contingency | BDT 5,000–10,000 |
| **Estimated monthly cash cost** | **BDT 65,000–95,000** |

Estimated one-time setup and launch cost: **BDT 20,000–40,000** when existing company resources are available.

Estimated six-month cash requirement: **BDT 410,000–580,000**.

### Scenario B: Dedicated acquisition support from Month 3

Adding one Fiverr/B2B lead-generation executive from Month 3 may add approximately **BDT 25,000–40,000 per month**, subject to hiring level and incentives.

Estimated six-month cash requirement: **BDT 510,000–740,000**.

### Recommended management reserve

- Minimum validation capital: **BDT 500,000**
- Recommended six-month approved ceiling: **BDT 700,000**
- Contingency ceiling for accelerated traction: **BDT 900,000**, released only after management approval

The six-month operating ceiling should be approved at the beginning so the branch can plan consistently. Finance may release it monthly or by implementation phase, while management reviews actual spending and performance.

## 11. Key Risks and Controls

| Risk | Management control |
|---|---|
| Insufficient market demand | Monthly offer and channel review, with formal repositioning after Month 3 if required |
| Dependence on one developer | Documentation, Git ownership, code review, and backup resource |
| Scope creep | Written scope, milestones, revision limit, and change requests |
| API and infrastructure cost overrun | Usage limits, alerts, and separate client billing |
| Client data or credential exposure | Central credential manager and access control |
| Marketplace dependency | Build Fiverr and direct B2B channels in parallel |
| Low-margin custom projects | Productized offers and project-level margin review |
| Cash-flow delay | Milestone billing, deposits, reserve, and marketplace clearing assumptions |
| Over-hiring | Capacity and pipeline gates before recruitment |
| Misleading profitability | Report both cash cost and fully allocated cost |

## 13. Six-Month Governance and Budget Release

Management approval will cover the complete six-month program. Budget release and performance review will follow three implementation phases.

### Phase 1: Months 1–2

- Budget allocation: up to **BDT 200,000**
- Purpose: research, developer capacity, demos, sales assets, Fiverr launch, and B2B launch
- Review focus: market response, qualified calls, proposal activity, and early paid work

### Phase 2: Months 3–4

- Additional budget allocation: up to **BDT 240,000**
- Purpose: project delivery, case studies, lead-generation support, productization, and infrastructure
- Review focus: delivery quality, project margin, testimonials, conversion, and repeatability

### Phase 3: Months 5–6

- Remaining operating allocation: up to **BDT 260,000**
- Purpose: channel scaling, recurring support, capacity expansion, SOP completion, and branch independence planning
- Review focus: recurring revenue, gross margin, pipeline stability, delivery capacity, and full P&L

The total planned operating ceiling across all three phases is **BDT 700,000**. Any spending from the separate contingency allowance will require additional management authorization.

### End of Month 6 decision

Choose one outcome:

- Scale as a dedicated branch
- Continue controlled validation
- Restructure the offers or market
- Integrate into another service department
- Stop further investment

---

## 14. Recommendation

Management should approve the complete six-month launch with an operating ceiling of BDT 700,000, released through the three implementation phases described above. The branch should begin with one Branch Lead, one AI Automation Developer, three narrowly defined solutions, three credible demos, and measurable Fiverr and B2B activity.

The branch should earn the right to scale through evidence: qualified demand, paid projects, successful delivery, acceptable margins, and recurring support revenue.
